SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION

I.	INTRODUCTION

1.1	PURPOSE

			This document presents an in-depth overview of the proposed Online Reservation and Booking Management System for Balar Hotel, located in Boac, Marinduque. It clearly outlines the system requirements by analyzing data collected from hotel staff, management, and customers. Both functional and non-functional requirements are specified to ensure that all stakeholders comprehend the system’s features, aims, and processes. The Software Requirements Specification (SRS) will act as a framework for the project, directing the development of a web-based reservation and booking management system. It emphasizes the operational difficulties associated with the existing manual booking process and provides a systematic approach for enhancing efficiency, minimizing errors, and improving customer satisfaction. This document guarantees that clients and users attain a clear understanding of the system’s capabilities and anticipated results

1.2	INTENDED AUDIENCE
	In line with the purpose of improving the hotel’s reservation process, the system is intended for the following users:
      1. Hotel Staff and Management

        This includes front desk staff, reservation supervisors, and hotel administrators who are responsible for managing bookings, guest information, and reservation records. The system will assist them in organizing reservations through a centralized digital platform, reducing errors and improving daily operational efficiency.



2. Hotel Guests / Customers
            These are individuals who wish to reserve or book accommodations at Balar Hotel. The system will provide them with a convenient and systematic way to submit reservation and booking requests online, eliminating the need for manual or informal booking methods.

1.3	INTENDED USE
	To ensure that the system fulfills the needs of its intended audience, the system is designed to be used as follows:
For Hotel Staff and Management

The system will serve as a tool for managing and monitoring reservation and booking requests, guest details, and booking records. It supports hotel staff in handling reservations and booking more efficiently while providing management with organized records that aid in operational oversight.

For Hotel Guests / Customers

The system allows users to submit booking or reservation requests anytime and anywhere through a web-based platform. This ensures a more convenient, accessible, and user-friendly reservation experience.

1.4	PRODUCT OBJECTIVES
	The objectives of the system are as follows:
1. To automate the hotel’s manual booking and reservation process through a centralized web-based system.
2. To reduce reservation and booking errors, miscommunication, and record duplication.
3. To improve customer convenience by allowing online reservation requests without manual coordination.
4. To assist hotel staff and management in efficiently managing booking records and guest information.
5. To reduce dependence on paper-based documentation and improve overall workflow efficiency.

1.5	PRODUCT SCOPE
	     Based on the identified users and intended use, the Balar iBook: Online Booking and Reservation Management System is scoped as a web-based application focused on managing hotel reservations in a centralized and organized manner. The system supports both hotel staff and customers by digitizing reservation-related processes.

	The scope of the system includes:
•	Online submission of booking and reservation requests by hotel guests
•	Centralized management of reservations and guest information by hotel staff
•	Digital storage and retrieval of booking records to minimize errors and lost data
•	Generation of basic reservation and occupancy records for operational reference

	The system excludes online payment processing, real-time room availability display, and integration with external financial systems. Its scope is limited to booking and reservation management to maintain clarity and reliability.

1.6	DEFINITION OF TERMS

TERMS	DEFINITION



Balar iBook	Balar iBook is a web-based online reservation system designed for Balar Hotel in Boac, Marinduque. It allows guests to submit reservation requests, track the status of their reservations, and receive confirmation notifications, while providing hotel staff with a centralized platform to manage, approve, or decline reservations efficiently.

Reservation	The process of booking a hotel room in advance, including guest details, room type, dates of stay, and any special requests.

Customer/Guest	An individual who books a room at Balar Hotel using the reservation system.

Front Desk Staff	Hotel employees responsible for handling room bookings, checking availability, confirming reservations, and coordinating guest requests.
Automated Notifications	A feature of Balar iBook that sends instant alerts to hotel staff or guests about new bookings, updates, or cancellations.

II.	OVERALL DESCRIPTION

2.1	User Need

		As technology advances, using digital systems in the hospitality industry has become more important for efficiency, accuracy, and customer satisfaction. However, based on the conducted interviews and survey shows that the current reservation process at Balar Hotel is mostly manual. It relies on phone calls, social media messages, email, and simple record-keeping. This method works well during normal times, but it has limitations during peak seasons. These include a heavier workload, delayed confirmations, and occasional loss or mismanagement of reservation records.
To address these challenges, the proposed Balar iBook: Online Reservation System will be used to provide a centralized web-based solution for the users. In-depth interviews with the hotel operations manager and surveys among the hotel staff and customers were conducted to ensure that the system meets the needs of the users. The needs of the users are discussed below:

Hotel Staff and Management

		The main target users of the system are the hotel staff and management, who are directly involved in the reservation process. According to the results, the hotel staff need a reservation system that enables them to handle reservations effectively and have access to accurate information about the guests. The system should minimize the need for manual reservation record-keeping to avoid problems such as lost files or late confirmation, especially during peak periods. Additionally, the hotel management needs a systematic way of organizing reservation information and summaries of hotel occupancy for monitoring, planning, and administrative purposes.

Hotel Guests / Customers

		The hotel guests/customers are the secondary users of the system and are directly impacted by the efficiency of the reservation and booking management system. The survey data shows that the guests/customers would like an online reservation and booking management system that enables them to make reservations and booking without having to make phone calls or use social media inquiries. The hotel guests/customers require organized information to enable them to reduce uncertainty and waiting time for the confirmation of their reservations. The instant notification of the reservation is crucial in enhancing trust, convenience, and customer satisfaction. The system should enable the guests/customers to make special requests during the reservation process.

By catering to the needs of both the hotel staff and the guests/customers, Balar iBook seeks to enhance the accuracy of reservations and booking, minimize the workload of the hotel staff, optimize communication, and offer a more organized and efficient reservation and booking experience that meets the existing and future requirements of the hotel.

2.2	Assumptions and Dependencies
In order for this Balar iBook: Online Reservation for Balar Hotel project to be successfully implemented at Boac, Marinduque, the developers assume that:

•	The management and staff of Balar Hotel have access to a stable and reliable internet connection (Wi-Fi, mobile data, or LAN) to efficiently manage room reservations, booking confirmations, and customer inquiries through the system.
•	Customers and potential customers have access to internet connectivity in order to submit reservation forms and receive confirmations.
•	The users (hotel staff and customers) possess basic digital literacy skills, such as navigating websites, filling out online forms, and checking email notifications.



•	The hardware used by the hotel staff (desktop computers, laptops) is fully functional and compatible with modern web browsers (e.g., Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge). Customers are assumed to use smartphones, tablets, or computers that support updated browsers and operating systems (Windows 10/11, Android 10 or higher, iOS devices, etc.).
•	The system complies with relevant Philippine laws and regulations, including the Data Privacy Act of 2012, ensuring proper handling and protection of customer data.

The functionality, performance, and availability of the system heavily depend on:

•	The effectiveness of these operations depends on the stability and strength of the hotel’s internet connection. Any interruption, slow speed, or outage may delay reservation processing, confirmations, and communication with customers.
•	The system’s accessibility and responsiveness depend on the customer’s internet reliability. Weak or unstable connections may result in incomplete submissions, failed bookings, or delayed notifications.
•	The usability and efficiency of the system depend on the user’s technical competence. Limited digital skills may lead to incorrect data input, misunderstandings of system features, or increased need for customer support.
•	The quality and compatibility of hotel staffs’ and guests/customers' devices and browsers have an impact on the reservation system's functionality and performance. Issues like obsolete hardware or unsupported software might cause delayed loading, errors, and unavailable functions.








III.	SYSTEM FEATURES AND REQUIREMENTS

3.1	Functional Requirements

For Hotel Guests / Customers:

1 The system shall enable guests to register for an account to use the online reservation facility. Inputs: Full Name, contact number, email, password, confirm password
2. The system shall enable guests to make room reservation requests using an online reservation form. Contents: room type, check in, check out
3. The system shall enable guests to provide personal details, reservation information, and special requests during the reservation process.
4. The system shall enable guests to view the status of their reservation requests.
5. The system shall have the capability to send reservation confirmation and status notifications to guests via email. 

For Hotel Staff and Management (Administrators):

6. The system shall enable hotel staff to view and process reservation requests submitted by guests.
7. The system shall enable hotel staff to approve or reject reservation requests.
8. The system shall enable hotel staff to manage reservation records and guest details. 
9. The system shall enable hotel staff to record and view special requests from guests.
10. The system shall enable administrators to produce reservation and occupancy reports for monitoring.
11. The system shall enable administrators to manage user accounts







3.2	External Interface Requirements

User Interface Requirements

•	The system interface should be simple, user-friendly, and easy to navigate for both guests and hotel staff.
•	The system should employ the use of clear fonts, buttons, and organized layouts to ensure ease of use.
•	The home page should display general hotel information, room details, and booking instructions.
•	A reservation form should be available for guests to enter booking information and special requests.
•	Administrative pages should only be accessible to authorized users via a secure login system.

Hardware Interface Requirements
For the system to accomplish its tasks, it must be able to interface with the following hardware:

•	Desktop computers or laptops with:
	- Intel i3 / AMD Ryzen 3 processor
		- 8 GB RAM
		- 256 GB SSD storage
		- Windows 10/11 or macOS
		- Updated web browser
		- Stable LAN or Wi-Fi connection






•	Smartphones or tablets for portable and quick online access by guests.
		- 3 to 4 GB RAM
		- 32/64 GB storage
		- Android 10+, iOS 14+, or iPadOS 14+
		- Update browser applications
		- Wi-Fi or Mobile Data Connection

Software Interface Requirements
For the system to accomplish its tasks, it must be able to interface with the following software:

•	Desktop computers or laptops with Windows 10 or Windows 11 operating systems for hotel staff and administrators.
•	A database management system such as MySQL for storing and managing reservation and guest information.
•	Email services such as Gmail for sending reservation confirmations and notifications.
•	Web browsers compatible with the system for online access.

Communication Interface Requirements

•	A stable internet connection for online access and communication.
•	The system shall employ the use of the Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) or the Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure (HTTPS) for internet communication.
•	The system shall comply with the Transmission Control Protocol / Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) suite for data transmission.







3.3	System Features

1. Homepage
Feature Name: Homepage
Description: This feature is the primary page of the Online Reservation System for Balar Hotel. It contains general information about the hotel and links to reservation-related features.
Outputs: Hotel information, room information, navigation menus, and reservation menus
User Interactions: Users can view hotel information and navigate to the reservation and account sections of the system.

2. User Account
Feature Name: User Account
Description: This feature enables guests to create and manage their accounts for accessing the online reservation system.
Outputs: Guest account details such as name, contact information, and login details
User Interactions: Guests can register, log in, and modify their account details. System Administrators can manage guest accounts.

3. Online Room Reservation and Booking
Feature Name: Reservation and Booking Room
Description: This feature enables guests to place room reservation or booking requests online using an online reservation form. Guests can enter their personal details, reservation or booking information, and special requests.
Outputs: Guest-submitted reservation or booking request containing guest details and reservation information
User Interactions: Guests can enter and submit reservation or booking requests online using the system.




4. Reservation and Booking Status Tracking
Feature Name: Reservation and Booking Status
Description: The feature enables guests to monitor the progress and status of their reservation or booking requests after they have been submitted.
Outputs: Status of the reservation request, which could be pending, approved, or declined
User Interactions: Guests are able to check the status of their reservation or booking requests.

5. Reservation and Booking Approval Processing
Feature Name: Reservation and Booking Management
Description: The feature enables hotel staff to evaluate and approve or decline reservation or booking requests submitted by guests/customers.
Outputs: The reservation or booking records are updated, and the status of approval or denial is also provided
User Interactions: Hotel staff are able to assess the reservation or booking requests and perform the necessary actions.

6. Reservation and Booking Notification
Feature Name: Notifications
Description: This feature is used to send notifications to the guests/customers about the status of their reservation or booking requests.
Outputs: Email notifications for reservation or booking confirmation or rejection
User Interactions: Guests/Customers are provided with reservation and booking updates via email communication.








7. Guest/Customers Information and Special Requests
Feature Name: Guest/Customers Special Requests
Description: This feature is used to store the guest/customers information and special requests provided during the reservation or booking process.
Outputs: Guest/Customers information and stored special requests
User Interactions: Hotel staff can view the guest/customers information and special requests for service preparation.

8. Reservation and Booking Reports
Feature Name: Reports
Description: This feature enables hotel management to produce reports concerning reservations and booking for monitoring and record-keeping purposes.
Outputs: Reservation, booking and occupancy reports
User Interactions: Hotel administrators can view and produce reports for management.

9. System Settings
Feature Name: Settings
Description: This feature enables administrators to control system access and settings.
Outputs: System settings and access levels
User Interactions: Hotel administrators can control user access and system settings.











3.4	Non-Functional Requirements

Performance
•	The system shall be able to efficiently handle multiple simultaneous reservation requests, account logins, and reports.
•	The system shall display the homepage, reservation form, and reservation status page within seconds under normal internet conditions.
•	The system shall process reservation approvals, rejections, and email notifications within a minutes after administrator action.
•	The system’s performance shall depend on the client’s internet connection speed and hardware specifications.
•	The system shall be capable of handling peak booking periods (e.g., holidays and local events) without system crashes or major slowdowns.

Security
•	The system shall implement robust security measures to protect customers’ sensitive data such as personal details, contact information, and reservation records.
•	The system shall use secure authentication (username and password) for hotel staff and administrators before accessing administrative pages.
•	The system shall not store confidential information such as passwords in browser cookies.
•	The system’s back-end database and administrative controls shall only be accessible to authorized personnel.

Scalability
•	The system shall be designed to accommodate an increasing number of customers accounts and reservation records without degrading performance.





Availability
•	The system shall be available online 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, except during scheduled maintenance.
•	The system shall be accessible using different devices such as desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

Usability
•	The system shall have a user-friendly interface design that is intuitive, easy to understand, and easy to navigate
